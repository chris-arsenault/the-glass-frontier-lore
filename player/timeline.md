# Timeline

Major events only. For full context on any event, follow the links. Dates are CE (Common Era, Earth-origin calendar carried forward by the colony).

## Era 1: The Ring Age (pre-2240 CE)

The before. Mostly mythologized. The elves were the dominant technical civilization for centuries, the orbital ring was the backbone of everything, and other species — including humans — arrived and were integrated into a functioning multi-species society. The details are hazy. The [Echo Rivers](cosmology/echo-rivers.md) carry fragments, but nobody alive remembers this era firsthand except, possibly, the [elves](concepts/elves.md).

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| pre-2000 CE | **Ring construction** | The elves design and build the orbital ring over centuries. A generational project driven by elven [resonance](cosmology/resonance.md) science and [ringglass](concepts/ringglass.md) engineering. |
| ~2050s CE | **Humans arrive** | Human colonists reach the Kaleidos system. The elves integrate them — and other arriving species — into ring-era civilization. |
| ~2100–2240 CE | **The High Ring** | Multi-species golden age. The system is connected, prosperous, and complacent. Nobody alive remembers a time before the ring worked. |

## Era 2: The Glassfall & Signal Famine (2240–2305 CE)

The catastrophe and its long silence. The ring shatters, communications collapse, and the system fractures into hundreds of isolated communities. During this period, the elves vanish. This is where procedural history begins.

|------|-------|-------|

| 2240–2305 CE | **The Signal Famine** | ~65 years of isolation. Surface settlements, ring habs, and outer-system worlds lose contact. Cultures diverge. ## Era 3: The Rekindling (2305–2340 CE)

The first generation after silence. Not a golden age — a raw, uncertain, sometimes beautiful period where scattered communities find each other again and discover how different they've become. The tone is first contact between neighbors. Some reunions are joyful. Some are bewildering. A few are hostile. But the dominant thread is people reaching out across the dark and finding someone reaching back.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2305 CE | **First long-range signals restored** | Surface settlements re-establish communication using salvaged and improvised resonance relay networks. The process is slow and unreliable. <ul><li>[future:Span Nine] — the first successful long-range relay; cobbled from salvaged ring-era components on a ridge between Sithari and Glasswake; becomes a landmark</li><li>[future:Sithari] and [future:Glasswake] — the two surface settlements that first reconnect</li><li>[future:Lattice Proxy Synod] — custodian AI data contributes to the relay design</li></ul> |
| ~2310s CE | **The Absence confirmed** | As contact spreads, it becomes clear that no settlement, hab, or outer-system colony has an elven population. Academic study of the disappearance begins. <ul><li>[future:Echo Ledger Conclave] — founded during this period to study the historical record and the gaps in it</li><li>[future:Vashtenri Thesis] — the first formal academic work proposing deliberate erasure (named for its author's family, decades before Lira Vashtenri)</li></ul> |
| ~2310s–2330s CE | **Ring hab contact** | Surface communities begin reaching ring habs. The cultural divergence is staggering — habs that went sixty years in isolation have become unrecognizable. Some welcome contact. Some refuse it. <ul><li>[future:Shuttered habs] — collective slang for habs that refused reconnection; some still shuttered in present day</li><li>[future:Hab Meridian embassy] — the first formal diplomatic exchange between a surface settlement and a ring hab</li></ul> |
| ~2320s CE | **Shear salvage begins** | Independent operators begin harvesting [ringglass](concepts/ringglass.md) from the debris field. Dangerous, unregulated, enormously profitable. <ul><li>[future:Coremark] — founded during this period; quickly dominates industrial-scale operations</li><li>[future:Shear] — the contested salvage zone between atmosphere and orbit</li><li>[future:Ratters] — slang for independent salvage crews; a subculture unto itself</li></ul> |
| ~2330s CE | **First inter-settlement trade networks** | Reliable trade routes re-established between major surface settlements. <ul><li>[future:Prismwell Kite Guild] — emerges to manage aerial trade logistics; kite-sail technology refined during this period</li><li>[future:Keel] — first major trade route re-established; connects Sithari to the Sable Crescent; named for the ridge it follows</li><li>[future:Kite-sail] — the resonance-powered trade vessel that makes long-range surface transport viable</li></ul> |

## Era 4: The Contested Reach (2340–2355 CE)

The cost of reconnection. When hundreds of communities that spent sixty years developing independently try to share resources, routes, and authority, some of those negotiations fail. The Contested Reach is not a single conflict — it's a generation of overlapping disputes, resource contests, and territorial clashes that erupt as the system knits itself back together.

The conflicts are real and sometimes brutal, but they are not the whole story of this era. For every dispute that turns violent, there are ten that get resolved over a table. The system doesn't collapse. It argues, bleeds, and stubbornly continues to rebuild.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2340s CE | **Salvage conflicts escalate** | Competition for high-grade ringglass in the Shear turns violent. Independent salvage crews, settlement militias, and ring hab defense forces clash over the most valuable debris fields. <ul><li>[future:Coremark] — their expansion into contested zones triggers the worst incidents</li><li>[future:Shardfall] — a specific flashpoint battle over a high-value debris field that kills dozens; galvanizes public opinion against unregulated salvage</li></ul> |
| ~2345 CE | **Corridor disputes** | Disputes over control of major trade routes between surface settlements. Several routes change hands multiple times. Civilian traffic is disrupted but never fully stopped — traders find alternate paths. <ul><li>[future:Prismwell Kite Guild] — their convoys are targeted; they begin arming escorts</li><li>[future:Keel] — primary contested corridor; control changes three times</li></ul> |
| ~2350s CE | **Ring hab standoffs** | Some ring habs that refused contact during the Rekindling now face pressure — economic, diplomatic, and occasionally military — to open their borders. Several forced-contact incidents trigger wider coalitions. <ul><li>[future:Shuttered habs] — some open under pressure; others dig in harder</li><li>[future:Coriolis breach] — a forced-entry incident that becomes a cautionary tale and rallying point for hab sovereignty</li></ul> |

## Era 5: The Silent Bloom (2355–2365 CE)

Something worse than war.

In the mid-2350s, a resonance anomaly cascades through a cluster of ring habs in the mid-drift. The cause is never fully established — the best reconstruction points to a chain of overloaded ringglass arrays in a salvage refinery that triggered sympathetic failures in neighboring fragments. The effect is devastating: uncontrolled resonance amplification that warps the crystal substrate of affected habs, rendering them structurally unstable and — in the worst cases — lethal to inhabit.

The Bloom is not sentient. It is not an attack. It is an industrial accident at civilizational scale — the consequence of decades of aggressive ringglass harvesting without the elven engineering knowledge that once kept the ring's resonance ecology in balance.

The Bloom cannot be fought. It can only be contained. And containment requires cooperation between communities that were, days earlier, threatening each other over trade routes and salvage rights.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2355 CE | **The Bloom ignites** | Resonance anomaly cascade begins in mid-drift ring habs. Uncontrolled amplification warps crystal substrate. Affected habs evacuated or lost. <ul><li>[future:Pyre] — [future:Coremark] refinery where the cascade originates; the name sticks to the ruin</li><li>[future:Bloom Zones] — the affected region; reality destabilizes</li><li>[future:Displacement] — mass evacuation; tens of thousands of Hab-Worlders lose their homes</li></ul> |
| ~2355–2358 CE | **Emergency coalitions form** | Contested Reach disputes shelved overnight. Rival factions pool resonance specialists, salvage crews, and resources to build containment cordons around affected zones. The coalitions are improvised, fractious, and effective. <ul><li>[future:Bloom Coalition] — the umbrella organization; draws from [future:Prismwell Kite Guild] (logistics), [future:Lattice Proxy Synod] (telemetry), [future:Echo Ledger Conclave] (resonance analysis)</li><li>[future:Lira Vashtenri] — gnome resonance engineer from the Conclave; develops [future:Lira's Wall], proving containment is possible</li></ul> |
| ~2358 CE | **[future:Verge Compact] established** | Salvage operators formalize the first system-wide regulatory body for Verge operations, born directly from the Bloom response. <ul><li>[future:Compact Charter] — the founding document; first system-wide regulatory framework since the Glassfall</li></ul> |
| ~2358–2362 CE | **Containment holds, coalition cracks** | The immediate crisis stabilizes. Without the existential pressure, the coalition begins fracturing along old lines. Disputes resume — now complicated by debts, favors, and grudges accumulated during the emergency. <ul><li>[future:Displacement Council] — forms from Bloom refugees; begins evolving from advocacy group into containment order</li><li>[future:Coremark] — publicly discredited; begins transition to criminal operations</li></ul> |
| ~2362–2365 CE | **The Bitter Reach** | The second wave of conflicts. Sharper than the first — the people fighting now have recent experience of cooperation, which makes the return to hostility feel like betrayal. But the Bloom proved something: when it mattered, they could work together. That memory doesn't prevent the fighting. It does limit it. Nobody wants to burn the bridges they just built. <ul><li>[future:Displacement Council] — stays neutral; focuses on containment; earns trust from all factions</li><li>[future:Dern Talish] — young cordon operator during this period; turns away armed convoys from three factions</li></ul> |

## Era 6: The Accord (2365 CE–present)

The current era. Born from exhaustion, pragmatism, and the grudging admission that the Bloom coalitions worked better than anyone expected. The Tempered Accord is not a government — it's a framework for mediation, a set of shared norms that most settlements have agreed to honor most of the time. It holds because the alternative is the Bitter Reach again, and nobody wants that.

The Accord era is young. The system is reconnected but not unified. The [Glass Frontier](cosmology/the-glass-frontier.md) is accessible, inhabited, and slowly falling apart. The Bloom zones are contained but not resolved. The elves are history. The future is being written by the people who survived everything else.

|------|-------|-------|
| ~2365 CE | **Tempered Accord ratified** | The Accord is formally adopted as the inter-settlement governance framework. Not a government — a mediation structure.
