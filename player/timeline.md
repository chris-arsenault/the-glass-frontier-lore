# Timeline

Major events only. For full context on any event, follow the links. Dates are CE (Common Era, Earth-origin calendar carried forward by the colony).

---

## Era 1: The Ring Age (pre-2140 CE)

The before. Mostly mythologized. The elves were the dominant technical civilization for centuries, the orbital ring was the backbone of everything, and other species — including humans — arrived and were integrated into a functioning multi-species society. The details are hazy. The [Echo Rivers](cosmology/echo-rivers.md) carry fragments, but nobody alive remembers this era firsthand except, possibly, the [elves](concepts/species/elves.md).

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| pre-2000 CE | **Ring construction** | The elves design and build the orbital ring over centuries. A generational project driven by elven [resonance](cosmology/resonance.md) science and [ringglass](concepts/ringglass.md) engineering. |
| ~2050s CE | **Humans arrive** | Human colonists reach the Kaleidos system. The elves integrate them — and other arriving species — into ring-era civilization. |
| ~2000–2140 CE | **The High Ring** | Multi-species golden age. The system is connected, prosperous, and complacent. Nobody alive remembers a time before the ring worked. |

## Era 2: The Glassfall & Signal Famine (2140–2305 CE)

The catastrophe and its long silence. The ring shatters, communications collapse, and the system fractures into hundreds of isolated communities. During this period, the elves vanish. This is where procedural history begins.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **2140 CE** | **[The Glassfall](history/events/the-glassfall.md)** | The orbital ring shatters. Cause unknown — suspected sabotage. Ring fragments drift apart; many survive as isolated habs. Surface infrastructure collapses. Communication severed system-wide. <ul><li>[Sithari](locations/settlements/sithari.md) — largest surface settlement, loses ring uplink</li><li>[The Shear](locations/regions/the-shear.md) — debris field begins forming</li></ul> |
| 2140–2305 CE | **The Signal Famine** | ~165 years of isolation. Surface settlements, ring habs, and outer-system worlds lose contact. Cultures diverge. Independent traditions of [resonance](cosmology/resonance.md) use develop. Isolated habs evolve unique cultures and governance systems. <ul><li>[Tuners](concepts/tuners.md) — first post-collapse resonance practitioners, working from fragments without elven theory</li><li>[Fermata Station](locations/settlements/fermata-station.md) — jazz governance solidifies during this period</li></ul> |
| ~2250s–2280s CE | **Disappearance of the elves** | Over the course of several decades, the [elves](concepts/species/elves.md) vanish. No settlements report elven populations when contact resumes. The historical record shows signs of systematic tampering during this period. <ul><li>[Echo Ledger Conclave](npcs/factions/echo-ledger-conclave.md) — later formed partly to study the gaps left behind</li></ul> |
| ~2260s CE | **Echo Rivers discovered** | Settlers on Kaleidos's surface discover that underground waterways carry [audible fragments](cosmology/echo-rivers.md) of pre-Glassfall transmissions. <ul><li>[Sable Crescent](locations/regions/sable-crescent.md) — region where the largest surfacing points are found</li><li>[future:Renn Duvasi] — the archivist credited with recognizing the voices weren't natural</li></ul> |

## Era 3: The Rekindling (2305–2340 CE)

The first generation after silence. Not a golden age — a raw, uncertain, sometimes beautiful period where scattered communities find each other again and discover how different they've become. The tone is first contact between neighbors. Some reunions are joyful. Some are bewildering. A few are hostile. But the dominant thread is people reaching out across the dark and finding someone reaching back.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2305 CE | **First long-range signals restored** | Surface settlements re-establish communication using salvaged and improvised resonance relay networks. The process is slow and unreliable. <ul><li>[Span Nine](locations/landmarks/span-nine.md) — pre-Glassfall hardline conduit; rediscovered during the Rekindling; connects Sithari to Pelhari and Threshold Station</li><li>[Sithari](locations/settlements/sithari.md) and [Glasswake](locations/settlements/glasswake.md) — the two surface settlements that first reconnect</li><li>[Lattice Proxy Synod](npcs/factions/lattice-proxy-synod.md) — custodian AI data contributes to the relay design</li></ul> |
| ~2310s CE | **The Absence confirmed** | As contact spreads, it becomes clear that no settlement, hab, or outer-system colony has an elven population. Academic study of the disappearance begins. <ul><li>[Echo Ledger Conclave](npcs/factions/echo-ledger-conclave.md) — founded during this period to study the historical record and the gaps in it</li><li>[future:Vashtenri Thesis] — the first formal academic work proposing deliberate erasure (named for its author's family, decades before Lira Vashtenri)</li></ul> |
| ~2310s–2330s CE | **Ring hab contact** | Surface communities begin reaching ring habs. The cultural divergence is staggering — habs that went sixty years in isolation have become unrecognizable. Some welcome contact. Some refuse it. <ul><li>[future:Shuttered habs] — collective slang for habs that refused reconnection; some still shuttered in present day</li><li>[future:Hab Meridian embassy] — the first formal diplomatic exchange between a surface settlement and a ring hab</li></ul> |
| ~2320s CE | **Shear salvage begins** | Independent operators begin harvesting [ringglass](concepts/ringglass.md) from the debris field. Dangerous, unregulated, enormously profitable. <ul><li>[Coremark](npcs/factions/coremark.md) — founded during this period; quickly dominates industrial-scale operations</li><li>[The Shear](locations/regions/the-shear.md) — the contested salvage zone between atmosphere and orbit</li><li>[future:Ratters] — slang for independent salvage crews; a subculture unto itself</li></ul> |
| ~2330s CE | **First inter-settlement trade networks** | Reliable trade routes re-established between major surface settlements. <ul><li>[Vantara](npcs/factions/vantara.md) — emerges to manage aerial trade logistics; KITE technology refined during this period</li><li>[The Keel](locations/landmarks/keel.md) — first major trade route re-established; primary orbital trade lane connecting ringglass production zones, Sithari, and far-end settlements</li><li>[KITE](artifacts/common/kite-sail.md) — the resonance-powered motive engine that makes long-range surface transport viable</li></ul> |

## Era 4: The Contested Reach (2340–2378 CE)

The cost of reconnection. When hundreds of communities that spent a hundred and sixty years developing independently try to share resources, routes, and authority, some of those negotiations fail. The Contested Reach is not a single conflict — it's a generation of overlapping disputes, resource contests, and territorial clashes that erupt as the system knits itself back together.

The conflicts are real and sometimes brutal, but they are not the whole story of this era. For every dispute that turns violent, there are ten that get resolved over a table. The system doesn't collapse. It argues, bleeds, and stubbornly continues to rebuild. The era spans nearly four decades — long enough for children born in the early disputes to fight in the later ones.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2343 CE | **[Shardfall](history/events/shardfall.md)** | A flashpoint battle over a high-value debris field in the Shear. Dozens killed. Galvanizes public opinion against unregulated salvage. <ul><li>[future:Drossmark Industries] — aggressive mid-drift extraction company whose methods endanger nearby habs</li></ul> |
| ~2351 CE | **Corridor disputes peak** | Disputes over control of major trade routes between surface settlements. Several routes change hands multiple times. Civilian traffic is disrupted but never fully stopped — traders find alternate paths. <ul><li>[Vantara](npcs/factions/vantara.md) — their convoys are targeted; they begin arming escorts</li><li>[The Keel](locations/landmarks/keel.md) — primary contested corridor; control changes three times</li></ul> |
| ~2358–2363 CE | **[Coriolis Breach](history/events/coriolis-breach.md)** | A forced-entry incident at a shuttered ring hab that becomes a cautionary tale and rallying point for hab sovereignty. Triggers wider coalitions among ring habs. <ul><li>[Shuttered habs](concepts/shuttered-habs.md) — some open under pressure; others dig in harder</li></ul> |
| ~2370s CE | **Overextraction reaches critical levels** | Decades of aggressive ringglass harvesting in the Shear without the elven engineering knowledge that once maintained resonance ecology. Resonance instabilities reported at multiple extraction sites. Few pay attention. <ul><li>[Coremark](npcs/factions/coremark.md) — largest operator; internal safety reports suppressed</li><li>[Pyre](locations/landmarks/pyre.md) — Coremark's largest deep-Shear refinery; pushing extraction rates to the limit</li></ul> |

## Era 5: The Silent Bloom (2378–2407 CE)

Something worse than war.

In the late 2370s, a resonance anomaly cascades through a cluster of ring habs in the mid-drift. The cause is never fully established — the best reconstruction points to a chain of overloaded ringglass arrays in a salvage refinery that triggered sympathetic failures in neighboring fragments. The effect is devastating: uncontrolled resonance amplification that warps the crystal substrate of affected habs, rendering them structurally unstable and — in the worst cases — lethal to inhabit.

The Bloom is not sentient. It is not an attack. It is an industrial accident at civilizational scale — the consequence of decades of aggressive ringglass harvesting without the elven engineering knowledge that once kept the ring's resonance ecology in balance.

The Bloom cannot be fought. It can only be contained. And containment requires cooperation between communities that were, days earlier, threatening each other over trade routes and salvage rights. The era lasts nearly thirty years — long enough for the coalition to form, succeed, strain, and finally dissolve without the underlying conflicts ever being resolved.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2378 CE | **The Bloom ignites** | Resonance anomaly cascade begins in mid-drift ring habs. Uncontrolled amplification warps crystal substrate. Affected habs evacuated or lost. <ul><li>[Pyre](locations/landmarks/pyre.md) — [Coremark](npcs/factions/coremark.md) refinery where the cascade originates; the name sticks to the ruin</li><li>[Bloom Zones](locations/landmarks/bloom-zones.md) — the affected region; reality destabilizes</li><li>[Displacement](history/events/displacement.md) — mass evacuation; tens of thousands of Hab-Worlders lose their homes</li></ul> |
| ~2378–2382 CE | **Emergency coalitions form** | Contested Reach disputes shelved overnight. Rival factions pool resonance specialists, salvage crews, and resources to build containment cordons around affected zones. The coalitions are improvised, fractious, and effective. <ul><li>[future:Bloom Coalition] — the umbrella organization; draws from [Vantara](npcs/factions/vantara.md) (logistics), [Lattice Proxy Synod](npcs/factions/lattice-proxy-synod.md) (telemetry), [Echo Ledger Conclave](npcs/factions/echo-ledger-conclave.md) (resonance analysis)</li><li>[Lira Vashtenri](npcs/heroes/lira-vashtenri.md) — gnome Coremark mechanic whose instinctive resonance work becomes the basis for containment; develops [Lira's Wall](artifacts/relics/liras-wall.md)</li></ul> |
| ~2383 CE | **[Shear Compact](npcs/factions/shear-compact.md) established** | Salvage operators formalize the first system-wide regulatory body for Shear operations, born directly from the Bloom response. <ul><li>[future:Compact Charter] — the founding document; first system-wide regulatory framework since the Glassfall</li></ul> |
| ~2384 CE | **[Displacement Council](npcs/factions/displacement-council.md) formalizes** | Evolves from Bloom refugee advocacy into a dedicated containment order. Takes over cordon operations from the Coalition. <ul><li>[Coremark](npcs/factions/coremark.md) — publicly discredited; begins transition to criminal operations</li></ul> |
| ~2384–2396 CE | **Containment holds, coalition strains** | The immediate crisis is over. Without the existential pressure, the Bloom Coalition fractures along old lines. Disputes resume — now complicated by debts, favors, and grudges accumulated during the emergency. The Displacement Council holds the cordons while the politics deteriorate around them. |
| ~2396 CE | **Bloom Coalition dissolves** | The formal end of the forced cooperation. The Coalition's infrastructure is divided among its members. The Displacement Council inherits the containment mission. The underlying Contested Reach tensions, shelved for eighteen years, remain unresolved. |

## Era 6: The Bitter Reach (2407–2423 CE)

The second wave of hostilities. Sharper than the Contested Reach — the people fighting now have recent experience of cooperation, which makes the return to conflict feel like betrayal. But the Bloom proved something: when it mattered, they could work together. That memory doesn't prevent the fighting. It does limit it. Nobody wants to burn the bridges they just built. Not completely.

The Bitter Reach is not one conflict but many — overlapping disputes that reignite across the system as the last institutional restraints of the Bloom era dissolve. It lasts sixteen years, long enough for a generation to grow up knowing nothing else.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2407 CE | **Hostilities reignite** | The uneasy peace of the late Silent Bloom era collapses. Old territorial disputes, resource claims, and political grievances — suppressed but never resolved — resurface across the system. |
| ~2409 CE | **Coremark completes criminal transition** | Under [future:Duthrek]'s leadership, Coremark abandons any pretense of legitimate operations. Becomes a criminal syndicate running Bloom Zone artifact smuggling from [future:Korvath]. <ul><li>[Coremark](npcs/factions/coremark.md) — now mafia-style organization</li></ul> |
| ~2407–2423 CE | **Multiple overlapping conflicts** | Salvage rights disputes, trade route control, ring hab sovereignty standoffs, resource blockades. No single war — a generation of recurring hostility. <ul><li>[Displacement Council](npcs/factions/displacement-council.md) — stays neutral throughout; focuses on containment; earns trust from all factions</li><li>[Dern Talish](npcs/heroes/dern-talish.md) — cordon operator during this period; turns away armed convoys from three factions</li></ul> |
| ~2418 CE | **Exhaustion sets in** | Informal truces begin appearing. The economic cost of sustained hostility, combined with the ongoing Bloom containment burden, makes continued fighting increasingly untenable. The people who remember the Bloom Coalition's success are now community leaders arguing for a framework. |

## Era 7: The Accord (2423 CE–present)

The current era. Born from exhaustion, pragmatism, and the grudging admission that the Bloom coalitions worked better than anyone expected. The Tempered Accord is not a government — it's a framework for mediation, a set of shared norms that most settlements have agreed to honor most of the time. It holds because the alternative is the Bitter Reach again, and nobody wants that.

The Accord era is young — fifteen years old. The system is reconnected but not unified. The [Glass Frontier](cosmology/the-glass-frontier.md) is accessible, inhabited, and slowly falling apart. The Bloom Zones are contained but not resolved. The elves are history. The future is being written by the people who survived everything else.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2423 CE | **Tempered Accord ratified** | The Accord is formally adopted as the inter-settlement governance framework. Not a government — a mediation structure. Signatories include surface settlements, ring habs, and outer-system representatives. <ul><li>[Tempered Accord](npcs/factions/tempered-accord.md) — the framework itself</li><li>[Lattice Proxy Synod](npcs/factions/lattice-proxy-synod.md) — architects the governance structure</li><li>[Vantara](npcs/factions/vantara.md) — architects the trade provisions</li><li>[Displacement Council](npcs/factions/displacement-council.md) — architects the humanitarian provisions; trusted mediator</li></ul> |
| ~2427 CE | **Verathi arbitration** | First major dispute arbitrated under Accord framework. The result satisfies nobody completely, which the Accord's architects consider a success. <ul><li>[future:Verathi] — the station or settlement at the center of the dispute</li></ul> |
| **2438 CE** | **Present day** | The Accord holds but strains. Tensions between expansion and preservation. Old grudges linger. Bloom Zones remain cordoned — sixty years and counting. New communities discovered in far-drift ring habs. The frontier is open. <ul><li>[Bloom Zones](locations/landmarks/bloom-zones.md) — containment ongoing; [Displacement Council](npcs/factions/displacement-council.md) stretched thin</li><li>[Coremark](npcs/factions/coremark.md) — criminal operations; artifact smuggling</li><li>[future:Far drift] — unexplored ring fragments being discovered; who speaks for newly contacted habs?</li><li>[Dern Talish](npcs/heroes/dern-talish.md) — current First Threshold of the Displacement Council</li></ul> |
