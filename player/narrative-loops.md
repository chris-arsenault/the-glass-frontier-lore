# Narrative Loops

Recurring patterns that generate stories. Adapted from procedural generation design — these are not mechanical rules, but structural patterns that should recur across the lore to create emergent narrative coherence.

Every loop follows: **NEED → PROGRESSION → CONSEQUENCE → NEW NEED**

---

## The Setting Acts

The world has agency. Resonance spikes reshape hallways. Echo Rivers surface voices unbidden. Bloom Zones expand. The Shear shifts its geography. Kite-sails work because physics obeys different rules near ringglass.

When writing, prefer constructions where the setting acts on characters:

| Instead of... | Use... |
|--------------|--------|
| Hero finds artifact | Artifact draws hero to it |
| Faction claims territory | Territory's resonance ecology shapes the faction |
| Tuner learns to sense | The resonance landscape teaches the Tuner |
| Engineer fixes the problem | The problem reveals something about the world |

This isn't mysticism. It's a world where the infrastructure has opinions because the infrastructure was built by people who understood dimensions that nobody alive can perceive.

## The Cultural Memory Loop

Events mark things. Things enshrine practices. Practices commemorate events. This cycle is how the world remembers itself.

```
EVENT (the Glassfall)
  → marks ARTIFACT (Glasswake's fallen hab fragment)
    → enshrines PRACTICE (the "First Signal" museum, tourist industry)
      → commemorates EVENT (the Reconnection)
        → marks new THINGS (Span Nine becomes a landmark)
```

The Echo Rivers are the literal embodiment of this loop — the ground remembers events as resonance imprints that surface as voices. The Conclave catalogues them. The catalogues shape policy. The policy creates new events.

**When writing:** Ask what this entity remembers, what marked it, and what practices grew from those marks.

## The Cooperation-Fracture Cycle

Groups cooperate under pressure. Pressure eases. Cooperation fractures. New pressure forces new cooperation. Each cycle leaves residue — relationships, debts, shared infrastructure, grudges — that shapes the next cycle.

```
CRISIS (the Bloom)
  → COOPERATION (Bloom Coalition)
    → RESIDUE (Verge Compact, relationships, debts)
      → FRACTURE (Bitter Reach)
        → NEW COOPERATION (Tempered Accord)
          → NEW RESIDUE (governance framework, unresolved Bloom)
            → [future fracture when the next crisis hits]
```

This is the system's political heartbeat. Every faction's present-day posture is shaped by which cycles they participated in and what residue they carry.

**When writing factions:** What cooperation did they participate in? What fracture? What residue do they carry? What would force them to cooperate again?

## The Knowledge Decay Loop

Knowledge is created, hoarded, lost, and rediscovered. The elves knew everything. They left. What remains degrades through imperfect transmission.

```
DEEP KNOWLEDGE (elven resonance science)
  → LOSS (the Disappearance)
    → DEGRADED PRACTICE (Tuners reinventing from fragments)
      → INSTITUTIONAL CAPTURE (Clarisant standardizes the fragments)
        → GAP DISCOVERY (someone notices what's missing)
          → PARTIAL RECOVERY (Echo River fragment, ring-era vault, Bloom Zone relic)
            → NEW KNOWLEDGE (different from what was lost, adapted to current context)
```

The Conclave sits in the middle of this loop — they can see the gaps but can't fill them. The Lattice Proxy Synod is further along — they've recovered partial knowledge from the AIs but can't understand the framework it sits in.

**When writing:** What knowledge does this entity have, and what's the decay chain? How many steps removed from the original source?

## The Corruption Progression

States progress through stages, not instant changes. This applies to physical corruption (duskgrain), institutional corruption (Coremark's decline from corporation to criminal syndicate), and cultural corruption (the Contested Reach's escalation from trade disputes to armed conflict).

```
HEALTHY → EXPOSED → COMPROMISED → CRISIS → [RECOVERY or COLLAPSE]
```

Examples:
- **Duskgrain:** Unexposed → mild use (pleasant) → sustained use (perception disorders) → severe (reality dissociation)
- **Coremark:** Legitimate corporation → aggressive but legal → negligent (Bloom) → discredited → criminal
- **Ring habs:** Stable → Shear encroachment → structural warning → evacuation or reinforcement

The "recovery or collapse" branch is the narrative choice point. Not everything recovers. Not everything collapses. The tension is in not knowing which way it goes.

**When writing:** Where is this entity on its progression? What would push it to the next stage? What would pull it back?

## The Prominence Oscillation

Entities rise and fall. A healthy world is not one where everything climbs to mythic. It's one where entities gain and lose prominence over time, and the oscillation itself tells a story.

- Glasswake was nothing, then briefly famous, then settled into unremarkable
- Coremark was powerful, then discredited, then criminal (different kind of prominence)
- The Conclave was niche, then the Bloom made them relevant, and they're not sure they like it
- The Tempered Accord is young and may prove fragile

**When writing:** Is this entity rising, falling, or stable? What would change that? Entities frozen at one level feel static. Entities with a prominence trajectory feel alive.

## Anti-Proliferation

From the Ice Remembers design: "Every system that creates entities must justify those entities' existence."

For our lore wiki, this means:
- **Fewer, more significant entities** — 50 well-connected entities beat 500 isolated ones
- **Relationships over statistics** — who knows whom matters more than population counts
- **No orphaned entities** — every entity should connect to at least one other entity via a typed relationship
- **No orphaned mechanics** — if an entry introduces a tension, another entry should respond to it

The graph's G7 orphan check enforces this mechanically. The lint's stale-future check enforces it editorially. But the real discipline is authorial: before creating a new entity, ask whether an existing entity could serve the same narrative purpose.

## How These Interact

The loops layer. A single event can trigger multiple loops simultaneously:

**The Silent Bloom triggers:**
- Cooperation-Fracture (Bloom Coalition → Bitter Reach → Accord)
- Knowledge Decay (elven knowledge gap → Bloom → partial recovery via Lira's Wall)
- Corruption Progression (Coremark healthy → negligent → discredited → criminal)
- Cultural Memory (Bloom marks the system → Displacement Council enshrines containment → containment commemorates the crisis)
- Prominence Oscillation (Coremark falls, Displacement Council rises, Lira Vashtenri rises)

When writing a pivotal event, trace its ripples through multiple loops. The more loops an event activates, the more significant it feels — not because you declared it significant, but because the consequences compound.
