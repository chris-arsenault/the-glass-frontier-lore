# Kite-sail

The general-purpose motive engine of the Kaleidos system. Kite-sails use kinetic-band [resonance](../../cosmology/resonance.md) to redirect opposing forces into a frequency null space, producing effectively unopposed momentum. A kite-sail doesn't fight drag, friction, or gravity in the conventional sense — it makes the opposition happen somewhere else.

They still have sails. The earliest models were literal sailing vessels with [ringglass](../../concepts/ringglass.md) arrays bolted to the hull, and modern designs retain sail elements — partly functional (the sail surfaces carry structural-band arrays for stability), partly aesthetic, partly because a ship without sails doesn't look like a ship to anyone raised in this system.

## How It Works <!-- How It Works -->

The core of a kite-sail is a kinetic-band ringglass array — a set of tuned crystals that generate a momentum nullification field. Within this field, forces opposing movement are shunted into a resonance null space. The vehicle moves as if nothing is pushing back.

Mid-bandwidth kinetic work. A kite-sail doesn't need a [Tuner](../../concepts/tuners.md) to operate, any more than a truck needs a mechanic to drive. Arrays are tuned at manufacture and maintained periodically. Fuel — [future:veilfire] for high-energy operations, ambient resonance for light duty — sustains the field.

The result is a vehicle that performs in atmosphere, vacuum, and the distorted environment of [The Shear](../../locations/regions/the-shear.md). The same engine that runs a surface route can lift to orbit, dock at a ring hab, and push into a debris field.

## Classes <!-- Applications -->

**[future:Flitters].** Autonomous local kite-sails — the robo-ubers. Short-range, single-passenger or small-cargo, operating within and between settlements. Ubiquitous. Taking a flitter is like calling a cab.

**Personal craft.** Sub-orbital or space-capable vessels owned by individuals. Upper-middle-class luxury — the equivalent of owning a nice sailboat. Comfortable enough for extended trips, not built for deep Shear or interplanetary hauls.

**Commercial haulers and passenger vessels.** The [Vantara](../../npcs/factions/vantara.md) fleet and independent operators. Cargo haulers running the [The Keel](../../locations/landmarks/keel.md) and other trade lanes. Passenger liners between major settlements. Fast couriers. This is the backbone of system commerce.

**Shear-rated vessels.** Salvage crews, mining rigs, survey ships. Carry additional structural-band arrays for reality stabilization shielding. The Shear's resonance distortion can interfere with kinetic fields if local physics aren't anchored.

**Military.** Warships and patrol vessels — remnants of the Contested Reach and Bitter Reach eras, maintained because piracy is ongoing. [Vantara](../../npcs/factions/vantara.md) operates the largest private armed escort fleet. Various factions maintain their own. Kinetic fields can be used offensively, which is technically possible and socially frowned upon.

**Capital class.** The largest vessels in the system. Luxury cruisers, battleships, mobile stations. Rare enough that most are individually named and recognized. These will likely become their own entries.

## Limits <!-- Limits -->

The momentum nullification field requires continuous resonance input. In resonance-rich areas (most of Kaleidos, the ring), this comes from the environment. In resonance-poor areas (deep interplanetary space, Shear dead zones), the vessel burns stored energy. Run out, and you're drifting.

The null space receiving the redirected forces is not well understood. It's a frequency domain, not a physical location. The forces don't "go" anywhere physics can track. This has never caused a problem in 130 years of use. Engineers find this more concerning, not less.

Large-scale momentum nullification — moving a ring hab — is theoretically possible and practically beyond any existing array's capacity.
