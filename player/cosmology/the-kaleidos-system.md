# The Kaleidos System

Kaleidos is one planet in a solar system with several. It's the most historically significant — the first colony, the cultural old world, the place the [ring](the-glass-frontier.md) was built around — but it is not the whole of civilization. Not anymore.

The system is accessible. With the right ship and the right knowledge, you can reach any body in it. Interplanetary travel isn't casual — it takes time, resources, and technology that not everyone has — but it happens daily. Commerce flows. People move. The system is connected, if unevenly.

## Kaleidos <!-- Description -->

The parent planet. Terrestrial, livable, and scarred. A century of glass dust settling from the shattered ring has changed the soil, the water, and the light. Crystal-laced rock feeds the [Echo Rivers](echo-rivers.md). Prismatic weather is normal. The surface is where most people live, spread across settlements that range from thriving trade cities to isolated homesteads.

Kaleidos is the old world — the cultural and political center of gravity. But "center" is doing heavy lifting. It's more like the place everyone has opinions about than the place everyone answers to. The planet's authority is moral and historical, not administrative. The settlements on its surface govern themselves, cooperate or don't, and look to the [future:Tempered Accord] for mediation rather than orders.

## The Ring <!-- Description -->

The [Glass Frontier](the-glass-frontier.md) itself. The shattered orbital ring and its hundreds of surviving habs. Technically in orbit around Kaleidos, practically a world unto itself — or rather, hundreds of small worlds, each with their own character.

## Other Worlds <!-- Description -->

There are major commerce hubs on other planets and moons in the system. Some are old colonies established before the Glassfall and never dependent on the ring. Others grew up after, as people spread outward from a broken center.

These worlds have their own cultures, economies, and politics. They trade with Kaleidos and the ring habs, send ships through ring commerce stations, and maintain their own relationships with [resonance](resonance.md) technology — sometimes quite different from what's practiced on Kaleidos.

The specifics vary. The system is big enough to contain multitudes.
