# Next Entities

Tracking note from the org-bootstrap breadth review on 2026-05-12.

## Findings

- The lore is already sufficient to support several party-org families besides
  Shear salvage: Bloom containment, Jazzocracy crews, Conclave field/archive
  work, Vantara logistics/escort crews, and surface mining/resupply.
- The bottleneck is uneven operational coverage, not total lack of ideas.
  Shear salvage has the best-developed "day one playable" stack: environment,
  material economy, hazards, work loops, and institutional context.
- Several adjacent domains are still missing key anchor entities, so a cautious
  generator will keep drifting back toward salvage.
- Some desired orgs are only medium-grounded today:
  - Coremark field teams are strongly implied but not concretely structured.
  - Conclave retrieval cells are plausible, but not directly authored as a
    field-team pattern.
- `world-seeds.md` contains useful expansion fuel, but it is lower-trust than
  complete player-facing entries for org bootstrap purposes.

## Why Salvage Wins

Shear salvage is the most complete operational domain in the bible:

- `player/locations/regions/the-shear.md`
- `player/concepts/ringglass.md`
- `player/npcs/factions/shear-compact.md`
- `player/npcs/factions/coremark.md`
- `player/locations/settlements/glasswake.md`

This stack gives a generator concrete answers to:

- what the crew does every day
- where they work
- what they use
- what can go wrong
- who regulates them
- who competes with them
- what the money is

Other domains often have a strong premise but weaker operational detail.

## Strong Enough Right Now

These lanes are already viable with moderate authorial confidence:

1. Shear mining / survey / salvage crews
2. Bloom containment teams
3. Fermata Station musician-political crews
4. Vantara route / escort / logistics crews
5. Surface mining and resupply crews around Glasswake

## Medium-Grounded Right Now

These lanes are plausible, but still ask Mara to invent too much structure:

1. Coremark strike / enforcement / extraction teams
2. Echo Ledger Conclave artifact retrieval or field-authentication cells
3. Clarisant field teams outside Kaleidos core
4. Threshold Station expeditions

## Next Entities

These are the highest-leverage additions if the goal is broader, more reliable
org bootstrap variety without trying to finish the whole bible.

### Priority 1

1. **Clarisant** (faction)
   - Needed because Tuners are one of the setting's strongest non-salvage
     professional identities, but their main institution is still a shell.
2. **Pelhari** (settlement)
   - Needed to give the Clarisant a real home base with daily-life and training
     texture.
3. **Threshold Station** (settlement / installation)
   - Needed to unlock Synod-facing crews, expeditions, proxies, and station
     operations as more than abstract background.
4. **Ratters** (concept, subculture, or faction-like social body)
   - Needed because independent salvage is repeatedly referenced but has no
     actual social texture of its own.

### Priority 2

5. **Deep Shear** (location)
   - Needed to distinguish ordinary Shear work from truly alien high-risk work.
6. **Cthonic beasts** (creature entry)
   - Needed because monster-hunting, escort, and containment crews currently
     lack a concrete bestiary anchor.
7. **Coremark field operations** (entry or sub-entry)
   - One concrete operational surface: extraction cell, enforcement crew,
     recovery team, or black-market convoy unit.
8. **Conclave field team model** (entry or sub-entry)
   - One concrete operational surface: retrieval cell, authentication team,
     Echo River expedition, or cordon-support archive crew.

### Priority 3

9. **Bloom Coalition** (historical faction)
   - Helps ground mixed-faction containment and response teams.
10. **Thornvault** (settlement / installation)
   - Gives the Conclave a real physical center, not just a name.
11. **Hab Meridian** (settlement)
   - Useful for hab-native orgs that are neither salvage nor surface politics.
12. **One additional ship-class entry besides KITE**
   - For example: survey kite, heavy hauler, escort kite, or ring-hab tender.

## Recommendation

Do not try to "finish the world." Add a small number of operational anchors.

If only six entities get written next, the best set is:

1. Clarisant
2. Pelhari
3. Threshold Station
4. Ratters
5. Deep Shear
6. Cthonic beasts

If two more fit after that, add:

7. Coremark field operations
8. Conclave field team model

That set should widen org bootstrap materially without requiring a full lore
completion pass.
