# The Absent — DM Truth

Public entry: [The Absent](../concepts/the-absent.md)

Everything the Redaction theorists suspect is correct, and the truth is worse — or better, depending on perspective.

## What They Are

The Absent are elves. A species native to the Kaleidos system, present long before the colony era. Physically similar to humans — taller on average, with elongated ear structure, lower bone density, and significantly longer lifespans (multiple centuries). The visual differences are subtle enough that an elf in a crowd can pass unnoticed unless someone is specifically looking. No advanced disguise technology required — a hat and a reserved demeanor will do.

They were the primary architects of the orbital ring. Not the sole builders — the ring was a genuinely multi-species project — but the resonance engineering, the crystal lattice design, the systems that made the ring function as a unified structure: that was mostly elven work. Their understanding of ringglass and resonance was centuries ahead of any other group in the system. Much of what modern practitioners treat as fundamental theory is, unknowingly, a simplified version of elven science.

## Why They Disappeared

The Glassfall was not an accident and not a simple act of sabotage. The ring was destroyed by an external force — the Adversary. The elves know what the Adversary is. Nobody else does, and the elves intend to keep it that way.

The elven leadership determined that the Adversary would return. When it does, the war will be existential — not a political conflict or a territorial dispute, but a fight for the survival of everything in the system. The elves' assessment is that this war cannot be won by conventional means and cannot be won quickly. It requires preparation on a scale of centuries, and that preparation cannot be visible to the Adversary.

The decision to disappear was deliberate, collective, and agonizing. The elves chose to sacrifice their place in civilization — their history, their credit, their relationships, their visibility — in order to prepare in secret. They judged that if the Adversary could find them, it could find and destroy whatever they were building. So they made themselves unfindable.

## The Scrubbing

The Redaction was executed during the Signal Famine, which provided natural cover. Communications were already severed. Records were already fragmenting. The elves used this window to systematically erase themselves from every archive, registry, and historical document they could reach.

The operation was vast and imperfect:

- **Written records** were the easiest. The elves had access to most major archives from their pre-disappearance positions. Authorship was stripped from technical documents. Participants were removed from historical accounts. Administrative records were excised. This work was done physically — agents visiting archives, editing documents, removing pages.

- **Echo Rivers** were the hardest. You can't easily edit resonance imprints embedded in planetary geology. Elven resonance specialists developed techniques to overwrite specific frequency patterns in the crystal substrate, replacing elven voice signatures with harmonic noise. The process was slow, imprecise, and couldn't reach deep deposits. This is why fragments survive — the deeper the river, the more likely a piece of elven transmission slipped through.

- **Living memory** was handled through time. The Signal Famine lasted sixty years. Elves who had been public figures simply didn't reappear when communications resumed. A generation grew up without them. Two generations, and they were folklore. Three, and they were an academic curiosity. The long elven lifespan meant individuals who remembered the decision were still alive to manage the transition; they watched themselves become myth.

## Where They Are Now

The elves are still in the Kaleidos system. Most live in hidden habs — ring fragments that don't appear on any chart, running dark, their resonance signatures masked. These habs are where the war preparation happens: weapons development, strategic planning, training, and the slow accumulation of resources.

A smaller number live among the general population, passing as human or as members of other species. These are intelligence operatives, supply chain facilitators, and — in some cases — people who simply couldn't bear to leave the world behind entirely. They maintain the Redaction by monitoring archives, intercepting discoveries that get too close, and occasionally nudging scholars away from productive lines of inquiry. The Echo Ledger Conclave's most sensitive Absent-related material has been stolen from their archives twice. Both thefts are unsolved.

## The Adversary

The force that destroyed the orbital ring. The elves know what it is. This document does not, because the details are not yet defined.

What is established:
- It is external to the Kaleidos system
- It is capable of destroying ring-scale megastructures
- The elves believe it will return
- The elves believe the coming conflict will be existential
- The elves have been preparing for this war for 130 years

## The Cultural Sacrifice

This is the emotional core of the elven story.

The elves didn't just hide. They erased themselves. Every contribution to ring-era civilization — the science, the architecture, the art, the governance frameworks — all of it is now attributed to "the builders" generically, or to other groups, or to no one at all. Elven children born in the hidden habs grow up learning about a civilization their grandparents built and then walked away from. They study resonance techniques their ancestors invented, now practiced across the system by people who have no idea where the knowledge came from.

This was a choice. The elven leadership chose cultural death over physical death, secrecy over credit, preparation over presence. They believed — and continue to believe — that the Adversary cannot be fought by a visible target. The only way to prepare for this war is to not exist.

Whether they're right is the question the story will eventually have to answer.

## DM Usage Notes

- The Absent public entry is written to be self-contained. Players should be able to engage with the mystery without ever learning the truth.
- The Echo River fragments are the primary discovery mechanism. An archivist PC or NPC who spends enough time on Absent material should start finding patterns that point toward intentional erasure.
- Elven operatives in the general population are potential NPCs. They are not villains — they're people making an enormous sacrifice for reasons they believe are justified. Some of them doubt. Some of them are tired. None of them are evil.
- The hidden habs are potential locations. What's inside them is an arms race — 130 years of preparation by the most technically advanced species in the system.
- The Adversary is a campaign-level threat. Don't define it until you need to.
