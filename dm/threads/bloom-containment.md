# Thread: The Bloom Containment

*An industrial accident at civilizational scale — the consequence of cheap ringglass, low caution, and a resonance ecology nobody understood well enough to protect.*

---

## The Emotional Core <!-- Description -->

The Bloom wasn't one catastrophe. It was the product of an environment — unrestrained mining driving oversupply, Span replication experiments made possible by cheap ringglass, a general sentiment toward progress that treated safety margins as obstacles. The specific incidents (Coremark's overload at Pyre, the Span replication fracture) happened near enough to each other to shatter already delicate space. But the real cause was a civilization reaching for capability it didn't have the knowledge to handle safely.

The emotional throughline is **responsibility recognized too late**. The people who built the Coalition weren't heroes who saw the danger coming — they were the same people who'd been ignoring it. The Bloom forced them to confront what their complacency produced. The cooperation that followed wasn't noble; it was guilty.

## Beat Sequence <!-- How It Works -->

### Beat 1: The Environment
**Era:** 2320s–2350s | **Filled by:** [future:Coremark], [Vantara](../../player/npcs/factions/vantara.md), [The Keel](../../player/locations/landmarks/keel.md)

The Rekindling's trade boom creates an environment of cheap ringglass and low caution. Coremark pioneers industrial-scale Shear salvage. Vantara builds trade infrastructure. Demand outstrips understanding. Nobody is studying resonance ecology because nobody thinks resonance ecology is a thing you need to study.

**Effect:** Ringglass is abundant, safety margins are thin, and the gap between what's being extracted and what's understood about the consequences is widening.
**Downstream:** Creates the conditions for both specific causes of the Bloom (Beats 2 and 3).

---

### Beat 2: The Span Demand
**Era:** 2340s–2350s | **Partially filled by:** [Span Nine](../../player/locations/landmarks/span-nine.md)

Span Nine gives Sithari communication capabilities nobody else can replicate. The political demand for Span-quality access grows during the Contested Reach — communication inequality is a grievance. Coremark, flush with cheap ringglass and engineering ambition, attempts to replicate the Span's properties.

**Effect:** A Span replication experiment pushes ringglass arrays into narrow-bandwidth territory that the operators don't understand.
**Downstream:** The experiment fractures local space near Pyre, compounding with Beat 3.

**Unfilled beat needed:** The specific Span replication project — who ran it, where, what they thought they were building.

---

### Beat 3: The Overload
**Era:** ~2355 | **Filled by:** [future:Pyre], [future:Coremark]

Coremark's Pyre refinery overloads ringglass arrays through aggressive extraction. The resonance ecology in the mid-drift region is already stressed by decades of unrestrained harvesting. The Span replication fracture and the Pyre overload happen close enough — in space and time — to cascade.

**Effect:** Sympathetic resonance cascade across mid-drift ring habs.
**Downstream:** The Silent Bloom.

---

### Beat 4: The Cascade
**Era:** 2355 | **Filled by:** [future:The Silent Bloom], [future:Bloom Zones]

Uncontrolled resonance amplification warps the crystal substrate of affected habs. Reality tears. Some habs become uninhabitable. Some become lethal. The Bloom Zones form — pockets where the resonance environment is so distorted that physics breaks down.

**Effect:** Tens of thousands displaced. Reality destabilizes across a significant portion of the ring.
**Downstream:** The Displacement (Beat 5) and the Coalition (Beat 6).

---

### Beat 5: The Displacement
**Era:** 2355–2358 | **Filled by:** [future:Displacement]

Mass evacuation. Hab-Worlders who lose their sealed environments — the one thing that defined their identity — become refugees in communities that didn't ask for them. Tension between refugees and host communities. Skills and culture from lost mid-drift habs scatter across the system.

**Effect:** A refugee population with nowhere to go and expertise nobody else has (they lived in the resonance environment that just broke).
**Downstream:** The Displacement Council (Beat 8).

---

### Beat 6: The Coalition
**Era:** 2355–2358 | **Filled by:** [future:Bloom Coalition]

The response doesn't form overnight — it takes days, maybe a week. The people who push for it are a mix: faction leaders already fed up with the Contested Reach who see an opportunity to unify, neutrals who recognize the scale of the danger, and — invisibly — elven operatives who leak the right information to the right people and provide logistical nudges (a trader offering free transport to the Bloom zones, a message transcript that reaches the right desk). [DM]

The Coalition draws from existing factions: [Vantara](../../player/npcs/factions/vantara.md) for logistics, [Lattice Proxy Synod](../../player/npcs/factions/lattice-proxy-synod.md) for resonance telemetry, [Echo Ledger Conclave](../../player/npcs/factions/echo-ledger-conclave.md) for historical resonance analysis, independent salvagers for Shear operations expertise.

**Unfilled beat needed:** The specific people who called for the Coalition — named individuals from different factions.
**Unfilled beat needed:** The elven nudges — specific moments of invisible intervention. [DM]

**Effect:** Contested Reach disputes shelved. Resources pooled.
**Downstream:** Containment becomes possible (Beat 7).

---

### Beat 7: The Turning Point
**Era:** ~2357 | **Partially filled by:** [future:Lira Vashtenri], [future:Lira's Wall]

Lira Vashtenri develops the first resonance containment device — Lira's Wall. This proves that the Bloom can be stopped, not just fled from. The Coalition gains momentum.

**Unfilled beat needed:** What was Lira doing when the Bloom hit? How did she realize containment was possible? The specific moment of proof.

**Effect:** Containment shifts from "impossible" to "difficult but achievable."
**Downstream:** The Displacement Council builds its containment mission around this capability (Beat 8).

---

### Beat 8: The Institutions
**Era:** 2358–2362 | **Filled by:** [future:Verge Compact], [future:Displacement Council]

The emergency produces lasting structures. The Verge Compact formalizes salvage regulation — the first system-wide regulatory body. The Displacement Council evolves from refugee advocacy into a neutral containment order. Lira Vashtenri founds the Council's containment operations.

**Effect:** Emergency measures become permanent institutions.
**Downstream:** These institutions become models for the Tempered Accord (via Reconnection thread).

---

### Beat 9: The Fracture
**Era:** 2362–2365 | **Filled by:** [future:The Bitter Reach]

The Bloom stabilizes. The existential pressure eases. The Coalition fractures along old lines — now sharpened by debts and grudges from the cooperation. Coremark goes underground as a criminal operation. The Displacement Council stays neutral and earns trust from all factions.

**Effect:** Second wave of conflicts, sharper than the first.
**Downstream:** Exhaustion motivates the Tempered Accord (via Reconnection thread).

---

### Beat 10: The Ongoing
**Era:** 2365–present | **Filled by:** [future:Displacement Council], [future:Bloom Zones], [Dern Talish](../../player/npcs/heroes/dern-talish.md)

Containment holds but the Bloom Zones are expanding slowly. The Displacement Council is stretched thin. Dern Talish leads containment operations and secretly cooperates with elven operatives. [DM] Coremark smuggles Bloom Zone relics — including duskgrain — through the black market.

**The open question:** Is containment sustainable? What happens when the Council can't hold the line?

## Thematic Cores Engaged <!-- Significance -->

- **"What do you build when the original blueprints are gone?"** — the Bloom happened because the resonance ecology knowledge was gone
- **"Just because you can reach it, should you?"** — Span replication + Coremark overreach
- **"Can you build something together while you're still fighting?"** — the Coalition
- **"Who decides what gets remembered?"** — the Conclave's resonance analysis was critical; the Bloom's causes are debated
