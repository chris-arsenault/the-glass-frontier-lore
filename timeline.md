# Timeline

Major events only. For full context on any event, follow the links. Dates are CE (Common Era, Earth-origin calendar carried forward by the colony).

---

## Era 1: The Deep Ring (~1400–2100 CE)

The elven era. Centuries of civilization before human contact. The elves are native to the Kaleidos system and the dominant technical civilization. The orbital ring is designed and built during this period — a project spanning generations even by elven standards.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~1400s CE | **Elven ring construction begins** | The elves begin engineering the orbital ring around Kaleidos. A multi-century project driven by elven [resonance](cosmology/resonance.md) science. |
| ~1800s CE | **Ring operational** | The orbital ring reaches functional completion. Integrated [ringglass](concepts/ringglass.md) lattice handles power, climate, communication, and automation across the system. Expansion to other worlds in the system accelerates. |
| ~2050s CE | **First contact with humans** | Human colonists arrive in the Kaleidos system. The elves integrate them — and other arriving species — into ring-era civilization. The colony becomes multi-species. |
| ~2100 CE | **Multi-species society established** | Kaleidos is a functioning multi-species civilization. The ring is the backbone. Elves remain the most technically advanced group but share governance and resources broadly. |

---

## Era 2: The High Ring (2100–2240 CE)

The golden age. Multi-species civilization at its peak. The ring is fully operational, the system is connected, and the colony is expanding. This is the period the Echo Rivers remember most vividly.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2100–2230 CE | **Peak civilization** | Multiple settlements across Kaleidos surface, fully connected through ring infrastructure. Other worlds in the system settled and integrated. Ring-era AI custodians active. Trade, culture, and governance at their height. |
| ~2230s CE | **Late ring era** | The system at its most prosperous and most complacent. The ring has worked for centuries. Nobody alive — even among the long-lived elves — remembers a time before it. |

---

## Era 3: The Glassfall & Signal Famine (2240–2305 CE)

The catastrophe and its aftermath. The ring shatters, communications collapse, and the system fractures into isolated communities. During this period, the [Absent](concepts/the-absent.md) vanish.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **2240 CE** | **[The Glassfall](history/events/the-glassfall.md)** | The orbital ring shatters. Cause unknown — suspected sabotage. Ring fragments drift apart; many survive as isolated habs. Surface infrastructure collapses. Communication severed system-wide. |
| 2240–2305 CE | **The Signal Famine** | ~65 years of isolation. Surface settlements, ring habs, and outer-system worlds lose contact. Cultures diverge. Independent traditions of [resonance](cosmology/resonance.md) use develop. Isolated habs evolve unique cultures and governance systems. |
| ~2250s–2280s CE | **Disappearance of the elves** | Over the course of several decades, the [Absent](concepts/the-absent.md) vanish from the system. No settlements report elven populations when contact resumes. The historical record shows signs of systematic tampering during this period. |
| ~2260s CE | **Echo Rivers discovered** | Settlers on Kaleidos's surface discover that underground waterways carry [audible fragments](cosmology/echo-rivers.md) of pre-Glassfall transmissions. |

---

## Era 4: The Reclamation (2305–2360 CE)

Reconnection. The long process of re-establishing communication, rebuilding trade, and negotiating coexistence among communities that spent sixty years becoming strangers. The system discovers that the elves are gone.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2300s CE | **Reconnection begins** | Long-range communication gradually re-established between surface settlements. Ring habs begin making contact. Trade routes reopen. |
| ~2310s CE | **The Absence confirmed** | As contact spreads, it becomes clear that no settlement, hab, or outer-system colony has an elven population. Academic study of the disappearance begins. |
| ~2320s CE | **Verge salvage industry emerges** | Organized operations begin harvesting [ringglass](concepts/ringglass.md) from the debris field. The [future:Verge Compact] forms to regulate. |
| ~2350s CE | **Tempered Accord negotiations** | Multi-year negotiations between surface settlements, ring habs, and outer-system representatives. Attempt to build a shared governance framework for a civilization that spent 60 years becoming strangers. |

---

## Era 5: The Accord (2360 CE–present)

The current era. The Tempered Accord provides a framework for cooperation, but unity remains aspirational. The system is reconnected, the ring is accessible, and the frontier is open. The elves are history — known, credited, and gone.

| Date | Event | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| ~2360 CE | **Tempered Accord ratified** | The Accord is formally adopted as the inter-settlement governance framework. Not a government — a mediation structure. |
| **2369 CE** | **Present day** | The Accord holds but strains. Tensions between expansion and preservation. The [Glass Frontier](cosmology/the-glass-frontier.md) is accessible, inhabited, and slowly falling apart. |
