# Jazzocratic Governance

A governance system in which authority is fluid, earned through demonstrated musicianship and — more critically — demonstrated listening. Policy is made through structured improvisation sessions rather than debate or vote.

The only known implementation is [Fermata Station](../locations/settlements/fermata-station.md), a ring hab on the [Glass Frontier](../cosmology/the-glass-frontier.md) where the system emerged organically from a bar that survived the [Glassfall](../history/events/the-glassfall.md) and grew into a self-governing station over 130 years.

## Core Principles

**Authority is performative.** You govern by playing. Political power flows to those who can hold a room — not through volume or virtuosity, but through the ability to listen, respond, and shape the direction of a collective improvisation.

**Law is repertoire.** The station's legal framework is encoded in jazz standards — pieces that carry accumulated political meaning from every significant session in which they were played. Calling a tune invokes precedent. Composing a new one proposes new law.

**Citizenship is auditioned.** Full political participation requires passing the Sit-In — a performance with the house ensemble evaluated not on technical skill but on the ability to listen and respond. The criteria are genuinely subjective, which is both the system's greatest strength and its most obvious vulnerability.

## Why It Works

The system selects for people who can read a room, compromise in real time, and subordinate ego to collective output. These are useful governance skills. The musical framework also makes power dynamics visible — everyone in the room can hear who's leading, who's following, and who's bulldozing.

## Why It Doesn't

The criteria for inclusion are aesthetic, not ethical. Beautiful resolutions aren't always just ones. The bureaucratic class (the Comp) wields invisible power. And the station's most essential workers (the Drone, who maintain the [resonance](../cosmology/resonance.md) frequencies that physically hold the hab together) are structurally excluded from governance because they can't leave their posts.

See [Fermata Station](../locations/settlements/fermata-station.md) for the full picture.
